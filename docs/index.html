Reader guide

Quick rules for reading the graphs

  • Green, positive, or above threshold usually means a modeled return is easier to detect or the interpretation is safer under the current assumptions.
  • Negative or below threshold means a return is weak, hidden, or riskier to interpret as a boundary.
  • False layer stronger than ocean means the model is warning about ambiguity, not proving a real false layer exists.
  • Lower corrected error means the Doppler correction helped in this controlled sensitivity model.

Research brief

False layers are the primary interpretation risk.

The site treats radar returns as a decision problem: a receiver sees multiple echoes, ranks their depth and strength, and can mistake an internal reflector for the real ice-ocean boundary.

01

Geometry biases the reading

Flyby altitude, side offset, topography, and delay change where a return appears to come from.

02

Dirty ice creates competing echoes

Internal briny or dirty layers can produce returns that arrive before the deeper ocean boundary.

03

The model keeps the claim narrow

Outputs are sensitivity tests for interpretation risk, not evidence that any specific Europa layer exists.

Graph / Model Audit

Graph QA checks whether rendered charts have titles, units, series labels, numeric values, and consistent scale conventions after live recalculation. OK means no visible issue was found; Review means a chart should be inspected before using it as evidence.

Formula Checks

Formula checks verify basic model assumptions such as positive inputs, valid toggles, chart-data links, and layer order. OK means the check passed, CHECK means the current controls may need attention, and REVIEW marks a caveat or item that should not be overinterpreted.

Model assumptions and limitations

Synthetic, illustrative, not mission-validated

  • Surface relief, internal layers, and dirty-ice structures are generated sensitivity cases, not measured Europa geology.
  • Echo power is relative dB: one-way attenuation is applied over a two-way path, then compared with a simple threshold.
  • The PRF value shown is a Nyquist-style Doppler sampling floor, not a complete radar PRF design rule.
  • Doppler depth correction is a controlled demonstration with deterministic residual error, not a full inversion from noisy instrument data.
